# Brief

Working title: Technical Whitepaper Tutorial

## Goal

Show how a compact technical document can move through Manuscript Lab's file
protocol without relying on private material or model provider secrets.

## Audience

New operators, contributors, and reviewers who want a concrete public example
before using the harness on their own documents.

## Constraints

- Use neutral public sample content.
- Keep the fixture small enough to inspect in one sitting.
- Avoid vendor claims, benchmark claims, customer data, private story details,
  model logs, and API keys.
- Demonstrate the workflow through durable files instead of chat-only prose.

## Success Criteria

- The fixture shows the role of brief, outline, style, draft, sources, state,
  issues, candidates, audits, and exports.
- The walkthrough covers init, compose, static check, review dry-run, issue
  flow, candidate dry-run, static diff audit, and Markdown/HTML export.
- A reader can copy the fixture over a fresh scaffold and understand which files
  are authored, generated, or sample-generated.
